<h2 style="font-size: 22px; margin-top: 40px;">A note on Comfort Fit</h2>
<p>Every Aydins ring is made <strong>comfort fit</strong> — the inside of the band is slightly domed instead of flat. This makes the ring slide on and off easier and feel less tight throughout the day. It also means a comfort-fit ring will <strong>feel about half a size larger</strong> than a standard-fit ring of the same number.</p>
<p>If you've previously been sized in a standard-fit ring, consider going down a half size for an Aydins ring. If your jeweler sized you specifically for comfort fit, use that size as-is.</p>

<h2 style="font-size: 22px; margin-top: 40px;">Pro Tips for an Accurate Size</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Measure when your hands are warm.</strong> Cold fingers shrink — measuring first thing in the morning or right after washing in cold water gives a falsely small reading.</li>
  <li><strong>Measure later in the day.</strong> Fingers swell slightly throughout the day. End-of-day sizing is closer to your average.</li>
  <li><strong>Account for your knuckle.</strong> If your knuckle is significantly wider than the base of your finger, size for the knuckle so the ring slides over — then expect it to feel a touch loose at the base.</li>
  <li><strong>Wedding bands run differently per hand.</strong> Your dominant hand is usually a quarter to half size larger than your non-dominant hand.</li>
  <li><strong>Between two sizes? Round up.</strong> A slightly loose ring is wearable. A too-tight ring is not.</li>
</ul>
