# Ring Care Guide

Your ring is built to last. Here's how to keep it looking like the day you got it.

---

## The short version

**Wear it. Live in it. Don't overthink it.**

Tungsten, ceramic, and titanium rings are among the most durable materials in jewelry. Showers, dishes, sleep, the gym — your ring handles all of it without complaint.

There are a few situations worth removing it for. Everything else is fine.

---

## When to take it off

**Take the ring off for:**

- **Heavy weightlifting** — gripping a barbell with your full body weight on it can pinch the finger against the ring (a phenomenon called "ring avulsion"). Not a ring issue, a hand-safety issue.
- **Beach sand, gravel, concrete work** — these can dull the polished surface over time. Doesn't damage the ring structurally, just keeps the finish sharp.
- **Heavy chemical exposure** — bleach, pool chlorine at high concentrations, industrial solvents. Brief exposure is fine; prolonged soaking isn't.
- **Mixed-martial-arts / contact sports** — anything where the ring could get crushed or your hand could get caught.

**Totally fine to keep it on for:**

- Showering, swimming, dishes, hand-washing
- Sleeping
- Day-to-day work — typing, driving, cooking
- Gardening, hiking, most outdoor activities
- Casual workouts, cardio, light lifting

---

## Cleaning your ring

Once a week, give it 30 seconds:

1. Warm water + a drop of regular dish soap
2. Soft toothbrush (an old one works perfectly)
3. Gentle scrub around the band and inside the ring
4. Rinse with warm water
5. Pat dry with a soft cloth

That's it. No special jewelry cleaners required. No ultrasonic cleaners needed.

**For polished finishes** — a microfiber cloth keeps the shine sharp.
**For brushed or matte finishes** — gentle wipe only; avoid abrasive cloths.

---

## Material-specific notes

### Tungsten

The most durable material in fine jewelry. Won't scratch under everyday wear. Won't bend or warp.

**Important:** Tungsten doesn't resize. If you ever need a different size, see the Lifetime Sizing Program below.

### Ceramic

Lightweight, scratch-resistant, hypoallergenic. Slightly more brittle than tungsten under direct hard impact (e.g., dropping onto tile from height) but in normal wear it's bulletproof.

### Wood inlay

The wood inlay is sealed and protected, but prolonged soaking can affect the seal over time. Quick showers and hand-washing are fine; don't leave it submerged.

### Stone / lava rock inlay

The stone is set with industrial-grade adhesive and is extremely secure. Avoid sharp direct impact on the inlay area to keep it pristine.

### Titanium / cobalt

Durable, lightweight, hypoallergenic. Treat the same as tungsten — wear it freely.

---

## Engraving care

If your ring is engraved, the engraving is laser-etched directly into the metal — it's not a coating or fill. It won't wear off.

The engraving sits below the surface, so the only thing that affects its appearance over time is dirt building up inside the etched letters. The weekly soft-toothbrush cleaning handles that completely.

---

## If the fit isn't perfect

Tungsten rings can't be resized in the traditional way — the material is too hard to stretch or compress.

**That's where our Lifetime Sizing Program comes in.**

If your ring fits when you get it but stops fitting later — weight change, finger size change, climate change — we'll replace the ring at a heavily discounted rate for as long as you own it.

- **After Year 1:** $54.50 to size up or down

We keep your engraving, your design, your finish — same ring, new size. As long as you own the original, the program is yours.

**Not enrolled yet?** Contact us at sales@shopaydins.com with your order number.

---

## If something happens to the ring

**Scratched the finish?** Tungsten and ceramic resist scratches under normal wear, but if something happens — message us. We have refinishing options.

**Lost it?** It happens. We'll help you replace it (often with a returning-customer rate). Reach out.

**Damaged inlay?** Wood or stone inlay damage is rare but possible from extreme impact. Send us photos at sales@shopaydins.com and we'll quote a repair or replacement.

---

## Long-term storage

If you take the ring off for a stretch (travel, extended hospital stay, long project):

- Store it in a soft pouch or the original box
- Keep it away from direct sunlight on wood/stone inlay (UV can fade certain wood species over years)
- Avoid storing loose in a drawer with other metal items (won't damage the ring, but can dull finishes)

---

## The bottom line

This ring is meant to be lived in.

It's not delicate jewelry. It's the everyday piece that goes through your life with you — the morning coffee, the long shifts, the workouts, the weekends, the milestones. Showers, sleep, work, play — wear it through all of it.

The small amount of care you give it (a 30-second weekly clean, taking it off for heavy lifting or rough work) keeps it sharp for years.
