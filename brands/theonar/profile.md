# Theonar — Brand Profile

## One-line positioning

**Titanium wedding bands built to outlast everything else you own.**

## What Theonar is

A power-built titanium ring brand for the guy who works with his hands, lives in a body that gets used, and wants a wedding ring that keeps up. Theonar is harder, lighter, and tougher than the polished jeweler experience. The product is the same caliber as Aydins — the personality is not.

Theonar exists because there is a real and distinct customer for titanium that wants a brand voice matching the material's strength-to-weight reality, not a refined jewelry-counter aesthetic. Tungsten customers go to Aydins. Titanium customers should land somewhere that feels like it was built for them.

## What Theonar is NOT

- Not a refined or jeweler-counter brand. That is Aydins.
- Not a budget marketplace brand. That is AmazingWeddingBands.
- Not a fashion brand. The ring is a tool, not an outfit.
- Not soft, warm, editorial, or storytelling-heavy.
- Not a lifestyle play (active grooms in mountain photography is too generic). Theonar is more raw than that.

## Target customer

- Age 28-45, blue collar or hands-on white collar (carpenter, mechanic, firefighter, paramedic, contractor, surgeon, plant engineer, military, anyone in a trade or anyone who lifts/builds/works with their hands)
- Has worn jewelry before and broken or damaged it. Wants a ring that survives.
- Picked titanium because tungsten felt like a brick on the finger, or because of nickel allergy, or because he saw titanium framed as "stronger steel."
- Values: function, durability, weight (low), comfort during real work.
- Doesn't shop a lot. Will buy once and wants it to be the right call.
- Buys for himself OR receives as a gift from a fiancee who knows him. Brand needs to feel right to BOTH audiences.

## Voice rules (Theonar-specific, ON TOP of universal rules below)

- **Short, declarative sentences.** Aydins gets to be poetic. Theonar gets to be blunt.
- **No fluff openers.** Start with the product fact or the use case. Skip "imagine wearing..." style intros.
- **Use second person and imperative voice.** "Wear it harder than you wear anything else." Not "It can be worn..."
- **Embrace the material's physical properties.** Strength-to-weight ratio. Scratch resistance. Hypoallergenic. Aerospace-grade. These are Theonar's poetry.
- **No softening words.** Avoid "lovely," "beautiful," "elegant," "stunning." Replace with "tough," "honest," "built," "real," "true."
- **No trust signals from Aydins.** Don't reference "since 2011" or any Aydins-specific heritage. Theonar earns trust through product spec, reviews, and policy clarity — not borrowed history.
- **Closes are tactical, not invitational.** Aydins closes: "Make it personal at Aydins." Theonar closes: "Forge it in. Wear it harder than you wear anything else." Or: "Built once. Wear forever."

## Visual DNA

### Color palette

**Primary:**
- Matte black: `#0A0A0A`
- Gunmetal: `#2D3138`
- Charcoal: `#1A1D21`

**Accent (pick ONE direction, not both):**
- **Direction A — Industrial Warm**: burnt copper `#B8623C`, ember orange `#D97742`
- **Direction B — Cold Steel**: cold blue `#3B6E8F`, oxidized teal `#2B4F4A`

Lock one direction during brand kickoff and stick with it. Industrial Warm reads more rugged-workshop. Cold Steel reads more clinical-precision. Both work; the choice depends on which customer subset you lean toward.

**Background:**
- Deep slate: `#0F1115` or near-black

**Anti-palette (do NOT use):**
- Soft warm beiges, creams, golds — those are Aydins' lane
- Pastels of any kind
- Pure white anywhere prominent (too clinical, breaks the rugged read)
- Aydins' purple/orange gradient
- Any pink or rose

### Typography

- **Display font**: Industrial condensed sans. Recommended: `Anton`, `Oswald`, `Bebas Neue`, or `Barlow Condensed Bold`. Tall, narrow, no-nonsense. ALL CAPS works for hero text.
- **Body font**: Sturdy sans-serif at higher weight than typical. Recommended: `Inter Tight Medium/SemiBold`, `DM Sans Medium`, or `Barlow Regular`.
- **NOT**: editorial serifs (Playfair, Cormorant), handwritten or script anything, anything labelled "feminine" or "luxury."

### Photography style

- **Light**: Hard directional, deep shadows. Single key light, no fill, no diffusion bouncing things soft. Aydins is moody-warm; Theonar is harder-cooler.
- **Surfaces / contexts**:
  - Raw steel plates with industrial texture
  - Concrete (poured, smooth or rough)
  - Distressed wood (workshop bench, not stylist-distressed bedroom dresser)
  - Leather tool roll, leather apron
  - Concrete + a single tool (wrench, chisel, etc.)
  - Inside of a clean toolbox
- **Avoid**:
  - White marble (Aydins-feminine)
  - Velvet ring boxes
  - Soft draped fabrics
  - Pastel anything
  - Flower or floral context
- **Human shots**: When a hand appears, it's a worker hand — calloused, scarred, or strong-looking knuckles. Not a soft-skinned model hand. Hand holding a tool, hand on a steering wheel, hand on a fire extinguisher, hand on a workshop vise.
- **Macro style**: Extreme close-ups on edge geometry, the inside finish, the engraving. Show the engineering.

## Universal rules (inherited from Aydins / CLAUDE.md — apply to ALL of Amir's brands)

These ALWAYS apply regardless of brand:

- **No em dashes (—) anywhere.** Locked rule from 2026-05-15. Use periods, commas, colons, semicolons, or parens.
- **No third-party brand names** (Thorsten, Universal Jewelry, JCK, competitors) in any customer-facing copy. White-label rule applies to Theonar too.
- **No bare "lifetime warranty."** Use "Theonar Lifetime Warranty" if referencing it. The exact policy mirrors Aydins (see policy mirror below).
- **No fabricated facts, statistics, or quotes.**
- **No "handcrafted/handmade/forged" claims about manufacturing.** Theonar engraves and ships in the US, doesn't manufacture from raw billet. Avoid implying it does.
- **Engraving conventions**: same as Aydins. Source page on universal-jewelry.com says "Engravable In Only" → inside-only tag. Says "Inside and Outside" → both. Match Shopify tag conventions exactly (`Inside` or `Inside & Outside`, no parens).
- **Comfort fit** is default unless specified otherwise. Always mention in Key Features.

## LOCKED 2026-05-28 (final picks)

Amir confirmed Theonar vibe is **Thor / Asgardian power god**, not blacksmith / craftsman.

### Accent direction (LOCKED)
**Cold Steel** (Direction B) — `#3B6E8F` as primary accent.

Reasoning: Thor's iconography is cold metal + storm blue + deep black + occasional gold detailing. Copper/warm would have evoked Hephaestus/Vulcan (forge god), wrong mythological energy. Cold Steel reads "thunder god / aerospace / cosmic metal."

### Display font (LOCKED)
**Anton** — `font-family: 'Anton', sans-serif`

Reasoning: Hits hardest of the four candidates. Tallest, widest, heaviest. Matches the "hammer landing" quality the brand promise requires.

### Body font (LOCKED)
**Inter Tight** — `font-family: 'Inter Tight', sans-serif`, weights 500-700 preferred for that "substantial" feel.

### Thor anchor (NEW)
The reference vibe is THOR — the Norse god of thunder. Use this as a creative test when in doubt:
- "Would Thor wear/use/buy this?" — if yes, on-brand. If no, off-brand.
- "Does this image/copy/color feel like lightning or like a blacksmith's apron?" — lightning is on, apron is off (lightning = Theonar, apron = wrong).
- Photography reference vibe: stormy skies, hammered metal textures, deep black backgrounds with single lighting strike, leather and rope props (Norse but not cosplay), mountain/peak imagery (Asgard energy).
- NEVER include: capes, horned helmets, runes pretending to be Norse, anything that feels like Marvel cosplay. The Thor reference is for ENERGY, not literal aesthetic copying.

### Brand voice samples (revised with Thor anchor)

Old: "Built to outlast everything else you own." (good, keep)
New supporting lines:
- "Forged for the strike, the swing, the grip."
- "Lighter than steel. Heavier than expectation."
- "Built for the storm."
- "Worn by those who build, not those who watch."
- "Titanium. Aerospace-grade. Earth-tested."

The voice should feel like the deity is speaking confidently about his own gear, not like a marketer trying to sell tools.
