## Pattern Analysis — What's Actually Wrong

### 1. Video Gap (Single biggest fix)
- **456 of 504 listings (90.5%) have no video.**
- Etsy 2026 gives ranking boost to listings with video.
- Fix: even a 3-second loop of the ring rotating in light beats nothing. Budget: phone shoot per listing during the photo refresh.

### 2. Tag Cannibalization (Structural)
Your top tags (with count of listings using each):
- "mens wedding band" — **351 listings**
- "tungsten carbide" — **312**
- "tungsten ring" — **290**
- "wedding ring" — **223**
- "wedding band" — **205**
- "tungsten band" — **176**

This means your listings are **competing against each other** for these searches. Etsy can only rank one of yours per query. The other 350 are wasted slots.

Fix: enforce "primary keyword diversity" rule in the optimizer skill — each listing must own a distinct primary phrase, not duplicate.

### 3. Photo Issues
- Etsy explicitly flagged the **Custom Logo Sterling Silver Signet** as "low-resolution primary photo." That's a $2k earner getting demoted for an easy fix.
- Your photos haven't been refreshed in 2-3 years per your own admission. Competitors moved to lifestyle/on-hand/scale shots. You're still mostly product-on-white based on what's visible.

### 4. The Starving Heroes Pattern
Several listings earned $4k-$30k+ all-time but now get 0-3 visits/month. This is the algorithm cycling them out due to:
- Stale photos
- Stale titles/tags (haven't been refreshed in years → algorithm sees lower relevance)
- Conversion drift (price changes, new competitors)

Fix: revamp these FIRST. Recovering a former hero is faster than building a new one.

### 5. The Hero Concentration Risk
**$101k of one SKU.** If anything happens to AYSSTAGP1 — Etsy strike, listing deactivation, dispute, copycat winning the keyword — your shop revenue collapses. Diversify by upgrading the other personalized/fingerprint listings (AYSSTAGP, AYTR005-CFP, AYTR011-CFP, JDTR800-CFP) to share the load.

### 6. Stock Anomalies
Two listings show **29,970 in stock** and **999 in stock** — these are clearly data entry errors or template defaults that should be reviewed. Inflated stock numbers can also affect listing presentation.
